About

David Orvek is a music theorist, pedagogue, guitarist, and composer in Columbus, Ohio. He graduated with a bachelor's degree in music theory and literature from Southern Adventist University in 2016, and is currently an MA student in music theory and a graduate teaching associate at The Ohio State University where he teaches first-year music theory and aural skills. His research focuses largely on post-tonal and atonal musics, with a particular interest in transformational theory. Current projects include a study of non-tonal triadic progressions in the music of Charles Villiers Stanford and a corpus study of closural features in the atonal music of Schoenberg, Webern, and Berg.

In addition to his musical interests, David is also an amateur computer programmer with experience in Python, Bash, Ruby, JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and the Humdrum Toolkit. This website was built from scratch using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
